Through excellent research and teaching, the University of Tübingen strives to find solutions to the challenges of the future in a globalized society. With around 200 degree programs, the university offers a wide range of subjects. The HRM and Organization department hereby specializes in topics of Personnel Economics under the leadership of Prof. Dr. Pull. I supported the department as research assitant for six months.

In my role as a research assistant, I provided comprehensive support to the faculty of HR Management and Organization across various areas, encompassing both academic lectures and research paper publications. This involved not only assisting in lecture preparations and administrative tasks but also actively participating in the research process itself. I contributed to literature reviews, data collection, and analysis, aiding in the generation of insights and findings crucial for the advancement of HR management scholarship. Moreover, I collaborated closely with faculty members to refine research methodologies, ensuring rigor and accuracy in our academic endeavors. This multifaceted experience not only honed my research skills but also deepened my understanding of the intricacies of academic research and publication. By actively engaging in the research process, I gained firsthand knowledge of the challenges and rewards of scholarly inquiry, further fueling my passion for academic pursuits.
